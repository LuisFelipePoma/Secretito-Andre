# 01 - Architectural Requirements

Goal: convert the client problem into prioritized architectural drivers.

## Input

- Phase `input.md`, derived from `project-brief.md` and prepared as a minimum briefing by the architect.
- Business problem, goal, scope/MVP, and stakeholders.
- Main features, expected quality attributes, and constraints.
- External systems, current context, and customer priorities.
- Selected tailoring profile and its rationale.

`architecture-drivers.md` is not manual input. It is the output generated by the agent from `project-brief.md` + `input.md`, then reviewed/approved by the architect.

## Process

1. Separate key functional requirements, quality attributes, constraints, and concerns.
2. Categorize quality attributes using ISO/SEI as base vocabulary.
3. Convert important attributes into measurable scenarios.
4. Prioritize with QAW-lite / utility tree and discuss tradeoffs.
5. Generate `architecture-drivers.md` with ASRs, scenarios, utility tree, and prioritized drivers.

## Output

| Artifact | Content |
| --- | --- |
| `architecture-drivers.md` | ASRs, measurable scenarios, utility tree, and driver list. |
| Approved driver list | Final list reviewed and approved by the architect. |

## Approval Gate

The architect does not manually write the whole document. The architect approves/corrects primary drivers, supporting drivers, metrics, priorities, and tradeoffs before moving to Architectural Design.

### Exit checklist

- Required inputs exist and critical scope questions are resolved or explicitly accepted.
- Requirements, quality scenarios, constraints, and drivers have stable IDs.
- Every primary driver has a measurable response or an explicitly accepted measurement gap.
- Profile, approver, approval date, and source versions are recorded.

Missing items block approval.

## AI Agent Role

The agent does the heavy analysis and drafting work: summarizes the problem, detects ambiguities, classifies ASRs, proposes measurable scenarios, prioritizes, and generates `architecture-drivers.md`. The architect keeps final decision authority.
