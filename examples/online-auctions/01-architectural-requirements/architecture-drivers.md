# Online Auctions - Architectural Requirements

Generated by agent, reviewed/approved by architect.

## Business Case

The auction company needs a nationwide online platform that preserves the urgency of live auctions while supporting aggressive expansion. Because of recent fraud allegations, the platform must make bid order, payment actions and participant history auditable.

## System Requirements

| ID | Requirement | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| OA-1 | Browse and choose auctions | Bidders find upcoming and live auctions. |
| OA-2 | Register bidder with credit card | Bidders create accounts and store a payment token. |
| OA-3 | Join live auction room | Registered bidders enter an active auction. |
| OA-4 | Watch live video stream | Bidders view the auctioneer through live video. |
| OA-5 | Place online bid | Online bidders submit bids during a lot. |
| OA-6 | Enter live room bid | Auction staff record bids placed in the physical room. |
| OA-7 | Publish bid feed | Participants see accepted bids as they occur. |
| OA-8 | Close lot and charge winner | System determines the winning bidder and charges the stored card. |
| OA-9 | Track participant reputation | Reputation index changes based on bidding, wins, payments and disputes. |
| OA-10 | Preserve audit trail | Bid, ordering, identity and payment activity remain reviewable. |

## Quality Attribute Scenarios

| ID | Attribute | Source | Stimulus | Artifact | Environment | Response | Measure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| QA-1 | Low latency | Bidder/clerk | Places a bid during a live lot. | Bid command path and bid feed. | Active auction under expected peak load. | Bid is accepted and published quickly. | P95 accepted bid visible to participants under 500 ms after server receipt. |
| QA-2 | Ordering/consistency | Online bidder and live room bidder | Place competing bids close together. | Auction sequencer and bid ledger. | Active auction. | Bids are accepted in the order received by the auction's command path. | 100% accepted bids have a monotonic per-auction sequence number. |
| QA-3 | Scalability | Nationwide audience | Thousands join a popular auction while other auctions run. | Real-time gateway, fanout and read models. | Peak nationwide load. | Active auctions remain responsive. | Support 1,000 participants per auction target and horizontal growth by auction. |
| QA-4 | Availability | Operations | Component fails during an active auction. | Live bidding platform. | Production auction. | Bidding path fails over or degrades without losing accepted bid order. | No committed bid lost; recovery objective under 60 seconds for active auction processing. |
| QA-5 | Security/compliance | Bidder/payment provider | Stores card and charges winner. | Payment integration. | Registration and lot close. | Card data is tokenized and charge actions are audited. | No raw card data stored; 100% charge attempts have audit records. |
| QA-6 | Auditability | Compliance/legal | Investigates disputed auction. | Bid ledger and audit store. | Post-auction review. | Ordered bids, actors, timestamps and payment actions can be reconstructed. | Complete immutable record for every accepted/rejected bid and charge attempt. |
| QA-7 | Modifiability | Business development | Onboards an acquired auction house. | Tenant, catalog and staff configuration. | Future rollout. | New auction house can configure auctions without changing bid ordering core. | Core bidding code unchanged for standard onboarding. |
| QA-8 | Observability | Operator | Reviews live auction health. | Metrics/logging/tracing. | Active auction. | Latency, participant count, bid failures and payment failures are visible. | Dashboards and alerts cover each active auction. |

## Constraints

| ID | Constraint |
| --- | --- |
| CON-1 | Browser-based participant UI. |
| CON-2 | Payment cards must be tokenized through an external provider. |
| CON-3 | Budget is not constrained. |
| CON-4 | Nationwide scale is a strategic goal. |
| CON-5 | Recent fraud lawsuit requires strong auditability. |
| CON-6 | Growth through mergers requires configurable auction-house onboarding. |
| CON-7 | Live video should use a managed streaming/CDN provider unless architects approve building one. |
