# Project Brief Template

## 1. Business Problem

What hurts, why it matters, and what happens if it is not solved.

## 2. System Goal

What the software must achieve in business terms.

## 3. Initial Scope / MVP

What is included and excluded from the first version.

## 4. Stakeholders

Users, sponsors, operators, maintainers, security/data owners, external systems, and decision makers.

## 5. Main Features

High-level stories, capabilities, or use cases. Do not prescribe architecture here.

## 6. Expected Quality Attributes

Performance, security, availability, maintainability, accessibility, scalability, observability, testability, deployability, and other relevant qualities. Include known measures when available.

## 7. Constraints

Required technologies, hosting, budget, dates, compliance, team skills, vendors, and decisions already imposed from outside the architecture process.

## 8. Current Context

Existing systems, codebase, integrations, technical pain points, shared data, dependencies, known risks, and prior decisions.

## 9. Environments And Operations

Development, test, staging, production, deployment, monitoring, support, recovery, and ownership expectations.

## 10. Customer Priorities

What is critical, what can wait, and which tradeoffs stakeholders consider acceptable.

## 11. Risk, Rigor And System Context

- Candidate rigor: Lite / Standard / High Assurance.
- System context: Greenfield / Evolution / Integration.
- Criticality, uncertainty, size, regulation, privacy/safety exposure, cost of failure, cost of change, and rationale for the selections.

## 12. Data, Security And Recovery

Data classifications, trust boundaries, threat/compliance concerns, SLO/SLI expectations, recovery objectives, migration needs, and rollback expectations.

## 13. Assumptions And Open Questions

State assumptions that may affect architecture and the questions that still require stakeholder or architect input.
