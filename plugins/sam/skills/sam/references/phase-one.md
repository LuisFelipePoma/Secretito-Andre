# Phase 1 Contract - Architectural Requirements

## Inputs

Read `project-brief.md` and phase 1 `input.md`. Confirm `Rigor profile` and `System context` (`Greenfield`, `Evolution`, or `Integration`).

## Drafting Rules

1. Assess sufficiency before proposing drivers.
2. Separate significant functionality, quality attributes, constraints, concerns, and assumptions.
3. Express material qualities with source, stimulus, artifact, environment, response, and measure or accepted measurement gap.
4. Prioritize with QAW-lite and a utility tree.
5. Propose primary/supporting drivers with source IDs, rationale, risks, and tradeoffs.
6. Keep technologies, patterns, and architecture choices out of this phase.

Stop only when a missing answer would change scope, a primary driver, a hard constraint, or a high-risk tradeoff. Otherwise record an owner, due point, or accepted risk.

## Approval Readiness

- The brief contains meaningful content, not template prompts.
- IDs are unique and sources are traceable.
- Each primary quality driver is measurable or has an explicitly approved gap.
- Critical questions are resolved, owned, or accepted as risks.
- Source hashes are current and every exit item is checked.
